Nasi Goreng

Nasi goreng is a popular Indonesian fried rice dish. The name literally means “fried rice” in Indonesian. It's typically made by stir-frying leftover rice with garlic, shallots, sweet soy sauce (called kecap manis), and a variety of other ingredients like eggs, chicken, shrimp, or vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 plates of white rice (preferably cold or leftover rice)
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 3 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • Cooking oil as needed
  • Optional toppings: sliced scallions, shredded chicken, sausage, crackers

steps

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan or wok.
  2. Sauté the garlic and shallots until fragrant and slightly golden.
  3. Crack in the egg, scramble until cooked through.
  4. Add the white rice, mix well with the seasonings and egg.
  5. Add sweet soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir well, pressing the rice to break up any clumps.
  6. Cook until the rice is evenly heated and the flavors are absorbed (about 3–5 minutes)
  7. Remove from heat, serve with your choice of toppings.
